Abstract

Due to their amorphousâ€like ultralow lattice thermal conductivity both below and above the superionic phase transition, crystalline Cuâ€ and Agâ€based superionic argyrodites have garnered widespread attention as promising thermoelectric materials. This study highlights the crucial role of lowâ€energy and overlapping optical modes in facilitating amorphousâ€like ultralow thermal transport, providing a thorough understanding of the underlying complex dynamics of argyrodites.

pmc1 Introduction

The search for crystalline solids with intrinsically ultralow thermal conductivity holds paramount importance in advancing the development of highâ€performance thermoelectric (TE) materials.[ 1 ] In addition to the extensive studies of conventional solidâ€state TE materials, current interest primarily focuses on superionic materials, exemplified by Cuâ€ and Agâ€based argyrodites.[ 2 ] These emerging argyrodites display substantial potential as TE materials owing to their exceptionally ultralow and temperatureâ€independent lattice thermal conductivity (ÎºL). Typical examples of Cuâ€ and Agâ€based argyrodites include Ag9GaSe6, Ag8SnSe6, Cu8GeSe6, and so on, characterized by the general formula Am+ (12â€n)/m Bn+X2âˆ’ 6 (Am+ = Cu+, Ag+, Bn+ = Al3+, Ga3+, Si4+, Ge4+, Sn4+, P5+, and X2âˆ’ = S2âˆ’, Se2âˆ’, Te2âˆ’).[ 3 ] These argyrodites are composed of a large number of atoms in the primitive cell and tend to undergo a phase transition from a lowâ€temperature ordered phase to a disordered superionic cubic phase upon heating to the critical temperature T c. In the superionic phase, Cuâ€ and Agâ€based argyrodites exhibit a hybrid structure featuring rigidâ€mobile sublattices. This structure consists of a rigid framework of BX4 tetrahedral, weakly bonded with Cu/Ag ions, along with partially occupied mobile Cu/Agâ€sublattices.[ 3 ] This disordered mobile Cu/Ag sublattice provides a considerable array of ion configurations with lowâ€energy barriers, promoting the migration of Cu/Ag ions within the crystal structure. Beyond their similarity in chemical compositions, complex crystal structures, multiple phase transitions, and disordered mobileâ€ion sublattice, Cuâ€ and Agâ€containing crystalline argyrodites typically exhibit an ultralow and temperatureâ€independent ÎºL, referred to as amorphousâ€like ultralow ÎºL, both below and above the superionic phase transition. Extensive investigations are currently focused on elucidating the origins of the intrinsically ultralow ÎºL observed in Cuâ€ and Agâ€based argyrodites.[ 2 , 4 , 5 ] The weak bonding force tends to cause a low speed of sound and high phononâ€phonon scattering rates,[ 4 , 6 ] which in turn govern ultralow ÎºL. Their complex crystal structures with large unit cells reduce the size of the Brillouin zone, and produce a small portion of heatâ€carrying acoustic phonons and a large portion of nondispersive and lowâ€lying optical phonons, yielding strong acousticâ€optical phonon scattering.[ 7 ] Moreover, the mobile Cu/Agâ€sublattices exhibit a pronounced interplay between complex lattice dynamics and highly mobile ions across the superionic phase. Recent inelastic neutron scattering (INS) experiments on Cu7PSe6,[ 8 ] Ag8SnSe6,[ 9 ] and Ag8GeSe6 [ 10 ] have revealed that the significant Cuâ€/Agâ€dominated lowâ€energy optical phonons were overâ€damped and facilitated Cu/Ag diffusions in the superionic phase. Over an extended period, the transverse phonons were anticipated to be suppressed across the superionic phase, and this suppression was expected to achieve ultralow ÎºL.[ 11 ] However, a recent INS study focusing on a singleâ€crystal Ag8SnSe6 sample has shown the persistence of longâ€wavelength transverse acoustic phonon across the superionic transition. Despite some investigations into the microscopic mechanisms underlying the ultralow thermal conductivity and the interplay between soft phonons and mobile ions in crystalline argyrodites, the TE community has initiated studies to understand the origins of amorphousâ€like thermal transport. Berngers et al. recently utilized a twoâ€channel lattice dynamics model to elucidate the observed temperatureâ€independent ÎºL of Ag8XSe6 (X = Si, Ge, and Sn) below the superionic phase transition. They identified that most Agâ€associated vibrations enable diffusive thermal transport,[ 12 ] indicating their nonpropagating diffusonâ€like character. Furthermore, Gupta et al. employed Greenâ€“Kubo simulations to reproduce the experimental amorphousâ€like ÎºL of a copperâ€based argyrodite Cu7PSe6 both below and above the superionic phase transition. They identified additional heat contribution channels from phonons, Cu ions, and their crossâ€correlations in the superionic phase.[ 8 ] Notably, the reported crystalline Cuâ€/Agâ€based argyrodites[ 4 ] have already exhibited anomalous amorphousâ€like ÎºL below the superionic phase transitions. However, a comprehensive understanding of the lattice dynamics and their correlations with the underlying amorphousâ€like ultralow thermal transports below the superionic phase transition in Cuâ€based argyrodites remains scarce.

In this study, we choose Cu7PS6, a Cuâ€based argyrodite, as a showcase material to explore the fundamental relationship between lattice dynamics and amorphousâ€like ultralow thermal transport below the superionic phase transition. Our synchrotron Xâ€ray diffraction (SYXRD) results enable the identification of the superionic phase transition temperature and Cu diffusion channels in Cu7PS6. Through experiments and theoretical simulations, we revealed the presence of Cuâ€dominated lowâ€energy optical phonons that strongly interact with acoustic phonons via avoided crossing, thereby governing their ultralow thermal conductivity, particularly in the nonsuperionic phase. Importantly, our simulations show a prominently dominant nonpropagating waveâ€like phonon transport in Cu7PS6 and demonstrate that Cuâ€associated lowâ€energy overlapping optical phonons play a crucial role in waveâ€like thermal transport in the nonâ€superionic phase. The computed values of ÎºL, obtained from both the Unified Theory (UT) and nonequilibrium molecular dynamics (NEMD), exhibit excellent agreement with the experimental ÎºL. In accordance with the heat conduction mechanisms proposed by Allen and Feldman for the amorphous or disordered systems,[ 24 ] the shortâ€range heat carriers with phonon mean paths either shorter than or comparable to the Ioffeâ€“Regel limit undergo waveâ€like diffusion through a Zenerâ€like tunneling between quasiâ€degenerate vibrational eigenstates. While in crystals with complicated structures, i.e., Cu7PS6, due to the substantial number of atoms per unit cell and the smaller minimum q waveâ€vector in reciprocal space, a multitude of optical phonon modes overlap within the crystal structure. Since the majority of these phonon modes possess comparable frequencies, exhibit low phonon group velocities, and engage in frequent collisions, the stored heat can efficiently transfer among those nonpropagated phonon branches through waveâ€like tunneling (analogous to Zenerâ€like tunneling in amorphous systems).[ 25 ] This contributes to an additional nonpropagating waveâ€like heat conduction channel, apart from the contribution of the propagating particleâ€like phonons using the phononâ€gas model within the framework of the Boltzmann transport equation.[ 26 ]

Considering the complexity and the significant overlapping optical phonons in Cu7PS6, the following quantitative assessment of thermal conductivity for the Cu7PS6 sample will consider the contribution of waveâ€like heat conduction. Based on the recently developed UT in complex crystals, which integrates waveâ€like and particleâ€like phonon transports in Wigner functions,[ 26 ] we have applied this framework to quantitatively analyze the thermal transport in the temperature range of 100â€“400Â K for Cu7PS6. It is important to note that within the superionic phase, complexities arise due to the accurate description of heat conduction in overdamped phonons,[ 25 ] convection heat transfer involving mobile Cu ions, and complex scattering interactions between vibrational phonons and mobile Cu ions, especially in complex materials. Hence, the quantitative assessment of temperatureâ€dependent lattice thermal conductivity for Cu7PS6 in the superionic phase (above 500Â K) falls beyond the scope of the current work.

According to the scheme proposed by Simoncelli et al.,[ 26 ] the KL comprises heat contributions from the conventional particleâ€like term (ÎºLp) and waveâ€like term (ÎºLc), as described by the following equation:[ 26 ] (3) ÎºL=ÎºLp+ÎºLc

The equation for ÎºL can be expressed as a square matrix, wherein the decoupled diagonal term and offâ€diagonal term represent the particleâ€like and waveâ€like components, respectively.[ 26 ] In anharmonic complex crystals, the interband spacing of nonpropagating waveâ€like phonons is typically much smaller than the phonon line widths. 3 Conclusion

In summary, our study explored the correlations between lattice dynamics and amorphousâ€like ultralow thermal transport through a comprehensive combination of experimental and theoretical methods. We successfully identified the superionic phase transition at â‰ˆ506Â K and directly visualized the Cu diffusion channel via SYXRD. In the highâ€temperature superionic phase, Cu ions exhibit a remarkably large diffusion coefficient of 5.5Â Ã—Â 10âˆ’5Â cm2Â sâˆ’1 at 550Â K through MSD analysis. Notably, the lowâ€energy optical phonons exhibit a strong anharmonic interaction with LA phonons through avoided crossing. We quantitatively examined the temperature evolution of KL from 100 to 400Â K in the framework of UT and NEMD. We found that significant dominant waveâ€like thermal transport in facilitating amorphousâ€like thermal conductivity in the nonâ€superionic phase of Cu7PS6. In a nutshell, our study provides an inâ€depth investigation of the microscopic mechanisms governing the amorphousâ€like ultralow KL in the nonsuperionic phase of Cuâ€based argyrodites. It demonstrates that Cuâ€associated lowâ€energy and overlapping optical phonon transport dominate amorphousâ€like ultralow thermal conductivity in Cu7PS6. We hope that our findings offer valuable insights to guide the rational manipulation of lowâ€energy overlapping optical phonons, thereby enabling controlled modulation of thermal transport in argyrodites for high TE performance.
